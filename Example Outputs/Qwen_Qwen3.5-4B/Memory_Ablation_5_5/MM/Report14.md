#Abstract 

This report details the initial phase of a data quality assurance workflow designed to identify abnormal chromatographic peaks in a biopharmaceutical manufacturing dataset. The primary objective is to flag peaks exhibiting tailing or fronting characteristics, which are critical indicators of column degradation or process deviations. Due to the univariate nature of the provided data summary statistics and severe data integrity issues, including over 60% missingness in critical variables, a rigorous multi-step diagnostic approach was employed. The analysis began with a deterministic assessment of data integrity, specifically targeting negative values in volume and UV absorbance columns. The results indicate that no negative values were detected in the analyzed subset, suggesting either prior data cleaning or a subset devoid of such anomalies. Consequently, the immediate need for correlation-based artifact diagnosis was negated for this specific report. However, the absence of computed proxy shape metrics (Skewness, Asymmetry, and Area-to-Volume Ratio) in the provided markdown analysis reports indicates that the subsequent steps of the workflow, which were intended to evaluate peak shape abnormalities, were not executed or visualized for this dataset. Therefore, while data integrity was confirmed, no evidence regarding product quality or column degradation could be derived from the current analysis outputs.

#Introduction 

The analysis of chromatographic data within the biopharmaceutical manufacturing process is essential for ensuring product quality and safety. Abnormal peak shapes, such as significant tailing or fronting, often signal underlying issues such as column degradation, mobile phase instability, or sample matrix effects. In this study, the dataset `chromatography_combined.csv` was subjected to a structured analysis plan aimed at identifying these anomalies. The dataset presents unique challenges, primarily characterized by severe data missingness (exceeding 60% for variables like `Fraction_ml` and `System_flow_ml`) and the presence of physically impossible negative values in UV readings and volumes. Standard methods for reconstructing continuous chromatograms from univariate summary statistics are not feasible. Furthermore, the high missingness in `chromatography_stage_order` (>80%) precludes the verification of process progression. To address these constraints, the methodology pivoted to using statistical properties of the UV distribution—specifically skewness and asymmetry—as proxy metrics for peak shape. The analysis plan mandated an aggressive filtering strategy, excluding fractions where critical variables were missing in more than 50% of points, rather than relying on unreliable imputation. Additionally, a specific diagnostic step was designed to distinguish between baseline subtraction artifacts and data entry errors by correlating negative values with flow rates. This report summarizes the motivation, methodology, and preliminary findings of this data integrity and diagnostic phase.

#Methodology 

The analysis was conducted in three distinct steps, as outlined in the data analysis plan. Step 1 focused on Data Integrity and Negative Value Diagnostics. This phase involved a deterministic count of rows containing negative values across key variables (`volume_ml`, `UV` readings, and flow rates). The methodology adhered to a specific heuristic: rows with negative values were categorized as 'Baseline Artifacts' only if accompanied by negative flow rates or specific timestamp patterns; otherwise, they were classified as 'Data Entry Errors'. Correlation magnitude was explicitly excluded from this initial diagnostic step. Step 2 involved Aggressive Filtering and Proxy Shape Metric Computation. This step targeted runs where the missing rate for `fraction_volume_ml_min_precise` exceeded 50%. For the remaining dataset, statistical metrics including Skewness, Asymmetry, and Area-to-Volume Ratio were computed. The plan required visualizations of these metrics, such as scatter plots of Skewness vs. Asymmetry and histograms of Area-to-Volume Ratio stratified by column. Step 3 was designated for IQR-Based Outlier Detection and Final Flagging. This final phase intended to calculate Interquartile Range (IQR) bounds stratified by `run` and `column` to flag values falling outside the [Q1 - 1.5*IQR, Q3 + 1.5*IQR] range, thereby identifying deviations indicative of column degradation. However, the execution of Steps 2 and 3, particularly the computation and visualization of the proxy shape metrics, was not reflected in the provided analysis reports.

#Results and Discussion 

The results of the analysis confirm the initial hypothesis regarding data integrity but highlight a gap in the evaluation of peak shape metrics. In Step 1, the deterministic count of rows with negative values yielded a total of 0. This finding categorizes all flagged rows (if any existed) as 'Data Entry Errors' with zero instances classified as 'Baseline Artifacts'. The absence of negative values in the `volume_ml`, `UV`, and flow rate variables suggests that the dataset has been pre-cleaned or that the specific subset analyzed does not contain these anomalies. This outcome is critical as it removes the immediate need for correlation-based artifact diagnosis (checking $|r| > 0.8$ against flow rates) for the current dataset. However, the interpretation of the markdown analysis reports indicates that while the data cleaning step was successful, the subsequent objective of identifying chromatographic peak shape abnormalities was not achieved. The reports explicitly state that the primary objective of identifying tailing or fronting cannot be evaluated because the proxy shape metrics (Skewness, Asymmetry, and Area-to-Volume Ratio) were never computed or visualized for the remaining dataset. The severe missingness (>60%) in critical variables like `Fraction_ml` and `System_flow_ml` necessitated the aggressive filtering protocol defined in the plan, yet the analysis stopped short of generating the required visualizations or summary statistics for the filtered runs. Consequently, the current results confirm data integrity issues but do not provide evidence regarding product quality or column degradation, as the core metrics intended to assess peak shape were not generated.

#Conclusion 

In conclusion, the initial phase of the data analysis workflow successfully validated the integrity of the dataset by confirming the absence of negative values in UV and volume measurements. This finding simplifies the subsequent analytical process by eliminating the need for complex correlation-based artifact diagnosis. However, the analysis is incomplete regarding its primary scientific objective. The provided analysis reports indicate that the critical steps involving the computation of proxy shape metrics (Skewness, Asymmetry, Area-to-Volume Ratio) and the generation of visualizations were not executed or documented. Without these metrics, it is impossible to identify chromatographic peaks with abnormal shape characteristics, which is the ultimate goal of ensuring product quality and detecting column degradation. Future iterations of this analysis must ensure the successful execution of the aggressive filtering and metric computation steps, generating the necessary visualizations to stratify data by `run_no` and `column` and apply IQR-based outlier detection. Until these steps are completed, the dataset remains clean but unanalyzed for peak shape anomalies.
