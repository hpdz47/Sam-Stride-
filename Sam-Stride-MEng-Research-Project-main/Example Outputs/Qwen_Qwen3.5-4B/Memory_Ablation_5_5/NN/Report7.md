#Abstract 

This report details the quantitative evaluation of chromatographic separation performance to ensure product purity and safety. The primary objective was to identify overlapping or poorly resolved peaks within the UV_1_280_mAU signal, defined quantitatively by a Resolution (Rs) value less than 1.5. To achieve this, a rigorous preprocessing pipeline was implemented, beginning with KNN imputation to handle missing context variables and data filtering to remove unreliable points. Subsequently, the raw absorbance signal underwent outlier filtering followed by Asymmetric Least Squares (AsLS) smoothing to correct systematic negative baseline offsets. Finally, Agglomerative Hierarchical Clustering with Ward's linkage was employed to group discrete fractions into peaks, stratifying the results by chromatography stage. The analysis successfully isolated peak shapes and stratified peak counts, though the current output lacks specific resolution metrics to definitively confirm co-elution events.

#Introduction 

In the context of chromatographic analysis, the detection of co-eluting analytes is critical for maintaining product quality and safety. Poor resolution, characterized by a Resolution (Rs) value below 1.5, compromises downstream fraction collection and can lead to the contamination of fractions with unwanted impurities. This study focuses on the evaluation of the UV_1_280_mAU signal to detect such instances. The dataset presents challenges, including significant missingness in critical context variables (Conc_B_ml, pH_ml) affecting over 65% of the data, necessitating robust imputation strategies. Furthermore, the raw data exhibits systematic negative baseline offsets and potential outliers that can skew baseline estimation if not addressed prior to peak analysis. The analysis aims to correct these systematic errors and utilize clustering techniques to group fractions into peaks, ultimately identifying specific retention volumes and stage conditions where separation efficiency is insufficient.

#Methodology 

The data analysis workflow consisted of three primary steps designed to preprocess the data and evaluate separation performance. First, data preprocessing involved KNN imputation to replace missing context variables using high-integrity neighbors, ensuring data integrity for optimization. Second, baseline correction and outlier filtering were applied to the raw UV_1_280_mAU signal. Outliers were filtered using Z-score or IQR thresholds to prevent skewing, followed by AsLS smoothing to estimate and subtract the baseline, isolating the peak shape. Third, peak clustering and resolution analysis were conducted. Discrete fractions were grouped into peaks using Agglomerative Hierarchical Clustering with Ward's linkage on Fraction_number and summed fraction_volume. Peak widths were calculated by summing volumes, and Resolution (Rs) was computed between adjacent peaks using the standard formula. Results were stratified by chromatography_stage to assess separation efficiency under different conditions.

#Results and Discussion

However, while the visual evidence suggests the method effectively isolates the peak apex, the absence of quantitative metrics regarding the AsLS parameters (e.g., smoothing factor lambda) and outlier filtering thresholds limits a comprehensive assessment of the methodological validity. Additionally, the lack of residual distribution data makes it impossible to fully verify the robustness of the baseline estimate against outliers. Regarding peak analysis, the data indicates a distinct bimodal distribution in peak counts per chromatography stage, with a high concentration of peaks at low retention volumes and a plateau in the mid-range. This suggests stable separation efficiency in the mid-range conditions. Nevertheless, the current textual output fails to explicitly display calculated Resolution (Rs) values or identify specific 'Problematic Peaks' where Rs < 1.5. Consequently, while peak clustering was successfully performed and stratified, the absence of resolution metrics prevents a definitive conclusion on whether co-elution compromises downstream fraction collection.

#Conclusion 

The data analysis workflow successfully executed the necessary preprocessing steps, including KNN imputation, outlier filtering, and AsLS baseline correction, to prepare the chromatographic data for peak analysis. The baseline correction visually confirmed the removal of systematic offsets, allowing for clearer peak shape isolation. Peak clustering via Agglomerative Hierarchical Clustering successfully grouped fractions and stratified results by chromatography stage, revealing a bimodal distribution of peak counts that suggests generally stable separation efficiency. However, the analysis is currently limited by the absence of calculated Resolution (Rs) values in the output reports. Without specific Rs metrics and the identification of problematic peaks (Rs < 1.5), it is not possible to definitively conclude which chromatography stages or retention volumes suffer from co-elution. Future iterations of the analysis must prioritize the inclusion of resolution calculations to fully assess separation performance and identify the specific conditions requiring method optimization.
