#Abstract 

This report evaluates the data integrity and structural quality of high-resolution HPLC and MS chromatographic time-series to determine suitability for automated feature extraction and predictive modeling. The analysis focused on three critical data quality dimensions: unit consistency and delta validation, temporal continuity and missingness patterns, and signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) feasibility. Results indicate that while UV absorbance signals are robust, critical process variables such as concentration (`Conc_B_ml`) and injection volume suffer from extreme non-random missingness (>60%), necessitating advanced imputation or exclusion strategies. Furthermore, a significant 77% missingness in `chromatography_stage_order` disrupts the hierarchical temporal structure, requiring specific gap alignment logic before retention time continuity can be assured. Unit ambiguity between `ml` and `mAU` columns was detected in specific runs, though no definitive resolution was attempted per protocol. The study concludes that while the core signal data is viable for feature extraction, rigorous preprocessing—including baseline correction, noise reduction, and hierarchical gap alignment—is mandatory before proceeding to fraction aggregation and predictive modeling.

#Introduction 

The objective of this data analysis workflow was to rigorously assess the integrity, completeness, and structural quality of chromatographic time-series data derived from High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) and Mass Spectrometry (MS) experiments. The primary motivation for this analysis stems from the requirement to distinguish between valid delta/offset measurements (e.g., negative values representing calibration offsets) and erroneous entries, while simultaneously addressing temporal discontinuities caused by missing metadata. Specifically, the dataset presents challenges regarding unit ambiguity between volume and absorbance columns, systematic missingness in critical process parameters like concentration, and a substantial 77% missingness in the `chromatography_stage_order` variable. These issues pose significant risks to the accuracy of retention time alignment, fraction aggregation, and subsequent predictive modeling. This report details the methodology employed to validate data consistency, assess missingness patterns, and calculate SNR feasibility, ultimately providing a comprehensive assessment of the data's readiness for automated feature extraction.

#Methodology 

The data analysis plan was executed in three distinct steps to systematically address data quality concerns. First, a Unit Consistency and Delta Validation step was performed on the `chromatography_combined_data.csv` file. This involved flagging rows with negative `volume_ml` or `Conc_B_ml` values as potential offsets and comparing the statistical ranges of `UV_1_280_ml` versus `UV_1_280_mAU` to identify unit confusion. Second, a Temporal Continuity and Missingness Pattern Assessment was conducted. This step calculated gaps in `stage_order` per run and stage, and evaluated the missingness of `Conc_B_ml` to determine if it was systematic (threshold >90%) or random. Third, an SNR Calculation and Fraction Aggregation Feasibility analysis was performed. This involved calculating the Signal-to-Noise Ratio on `UV_260_mAU` and `UV_280_mAU` after removing negative offsets, aggregating signals by `Fraction_number`, and reporting the percentage of data lost due to missing fraction metadata. All outputs were consolidated into markdown reports detailing counts, statistical summaries, and boolean flags regarding data feasibility.

#Results and Discussion 

The analysis revealed significant data quality issues that require specific handling before predictive modeling can proceed. Regarding unit consistency, the validation of `UV_1_280_ml` versus `UV_1_280_mAU` indicated overlapping ranges in certain runs, triggering a 'unit_ambiguous' flag. While negative values in `volume_ml` and `Conc_B_ml` were identified and flagged as potential offsets, no resolution was attempted, adhering to the protocol to avoid falsifying data. Temporal continuity assessment highlighted a critical disruption: 77% of `chromatography_stage_order` entries were missing. This non-random missingness, combined with over 60% missingness in `Conc_B_ml`, suggests a systematic failure in metadata capture rather than random noise. The SNR feasibility analysis confirmed that despite these metadata gaps, the UV absorbance signals (`UV_260_mAU`, `UV_280_mAU`) retained sufficient signal strength, with a majority of stages achieving an average SNR greater than 10. However, the high percentage of data lost due to missing `Fraction_number` indicates that direct aggregation without preprocessing is not feasible. The combination of these findings supports the conclusion that the data is robust for feature extraction only after implementing specific preprocessing steps, including baseline correction, noise reduction, and hierarchical gap alignment to restore temporal continuity.

#Conclusion 

In conclusion, the data analysis workflow successfully identified that while the core UV absorbance signals are robust enough for automated feature extraction, the dataset suffers from severe structural and metadata deficiencies. The 77% missingness in `chromatography_stage_order` and the >60% missingness in `Conc_B_ml` create significant barriers to accurate retention time alignment and yield/purity modeling. Unit ambiguities and negative offset values were detected but left unresolved per protocol. To proceed with predictive modeling, it is imperative to implement advanced preprocessing strategies, including the resolution of temporal gaps through hierarchical alignment, advanced imputation for missing process parameters, and specific handling of unit ambiguities. Without these interventions, any subsequent analysis will be compromised by structural discontinuities and missing critical process variables.
